## Summary

**Proceed with conditions.** With the answers above, two of the four original launch-blockers drop and one re-shapes. The architecture remains defensible (EU hosting, processor-on-artist's-behalf model, cookie-light analytics). What remains:

**Launch-blocking (must fix before onboarding the first real artist):**

1. **No public Data Processing Agreement.** Artists are controllers of client booking-request data; Inklee is the processor. Without a DPA, Article 28 GDPR is not met. Currently mitigated by the fact that `/ouchy` is a demo account — so no real third-party data sits in the gap today — but this becomes a live exposure the moment the first real artist signs up.
2. **Live `/terms` is too thin and inconsistent with the live feature set.** No eligibility, no artist responsibility for age/health/safety, no deposit clause, no warranty disclaimer, no liability cap, no governing law, no DPA reference, no change-notification clause. Also still says "beta period" while the product is GA.
3. **Imprint still needs publishing fixes.** The data exists (Michel Kräft as management-board representative; "Not VAT-registered" disclosure). It now needs to be added to the live `/imprint` page, along with a DSA Art. 11/12 single-point-of-contact line.
4. **Required deposit wording is not on any surface where deposits are presented.** Stripe is live, but the exact line — "Inklee is built to make deposits part of the booking flow. Availability depends on your current setup and enabled features." — must appear in artist deposit settings, on the public booking page where a deposit is requested, and in any marketing-site copy that mentions deposits.

**Next-sprint fixes:**

- Replace `/privacy` with a counsel-reviewed Section 4: legal bases per activity, controller/processor split, full subprocessor list (Stripe, Sentry, Upstash, Cloudflare, Google OAuth now included), international transfers, minors, UK GDPR, magic-link disclosure, breach commitment, AKI/ICO complaint rights.
- Publish a dedicated `/cookies` page.
- Publish a public Acceptable Use Policy at `/acceptable-use`.
- Publish `/subprocessors`.
- Stand up a DSA notice-and-action route (`/legal/report` or `/dsa`).
- Replace the homepage "GDPR compliant" badge with a verifiable statement (e.g. "EU-hosted, GDPR-aligned") unless a third-party assessment can back it.
- Track Estonian VAT threshold and re-issue the imprint update when registration is required.
- Ship a DPIA covering booking-image processing, magic-link tokens, and the Stripe deposit flow.

## Further Review Recommended

The following items are beyond the scope of a self-serve compliance check and should be reviewed by qualified counsel or specialists:

- **Estonian Information Society Services Act §4 imprint review**, and confirmation of any sector-specific disclosure requirements for tattoo-adjacent platforms.
- **Controller vs. joint-controller framing** for analytics, security logs, abuse handling, and the platform-defined 30-day deletion job. If a regulator characterises Inklee as a joint controller for any of these, the DPA needs an Art. 26 arrangement section, not an Art. 28 framing.
- **PSD2 / PSD3 analysis** for the Stripe Connect deposit flow.
- **Consumer-vs-business classification of sole-trader artists** under Estonian Law of Obligations Act and EU Consumer Rights Directive. This affects: liability cap, change-of-terms procedure, withdrawal rights when paid plans launch, and B2C-style cancellation flows.
- **DSA scope** — confirm whether Inklee falls within Art. 19 micro/small-enterprise exemptions and which Art. 11/12/14/16/17/25 obligations apply.
- **DPIA review** for booking-image processing and any future Studio multi-user flows.
- **International transfers** — independent legal review of EU-US transfer mechanism for Stripe, Sentry, and any other US-touching subprocessor; readiness for a post-DPF challenge scenario.
- **Trademark clearance** for "Inklee" and "inkl.ee" in target jurisdictions.
- **Marketing claim substantiation** for the "GDPR compliant" badge and the Stripe deposit feature card.
