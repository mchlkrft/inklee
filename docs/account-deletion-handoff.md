# Account Deletion — Data-Protection Position & Implementation Requirements

**Entity:** Inklee OÜ (Estonia) ("Inklee" or the "Platform")
**Subject:** In-app account deletion for Artists, and the treatment of associated Client data, financial records, and Stripe Connect data
**Date:** 2026-06-08
**Companion materials:** `docs/account-deletion-for-counsel.md` (feature description), `docs/payment-flow-for-counsel.md` (deposit / merchant-of-record model), `docs/account-deletion-design-2026-06-08.md` (data-flow detail)

**Defined terms.** "Artist" — a registered user of the Platform. "Client" — an Artist's
customer whose personal data is collected through the Platform. "Platform Fee" — the
3% application fee Inklee retains on each in-app deposit, being Inklee's own revenue.
"Connected Account" — the Artist's Stripe Connect (Custom) account. "Deposit" — the
sum paid by a Client to an Artist, for which the Artist is merchant of record.

---

## 1. Roles and controllership

Inklee OÜ is the data controller for: (a) Artist account and identity data (name,
email, Instagram handle, bio, location, profile and cover images, authentication
email, push tokens); (b) Client data that Inklee processes for its own purposes,
including booking servicing, platform security, audit and abuse prevention, and its
own Platform Fee and accounting records; and (c) technical and audit data generated by
the Platform.

For Client material that Inklee processes solely on an Artist's behalf and does not use
for any purpose of its own — namely the Artist's private Client notes and Client-uploaded
reference images — Inklee acts as processor and the Artist is the controller. The
processing terms required by Article 28 GDPR (processing only on documented
instruction, confidentiality, security, sub-processing, assistance, and deletion or
return on termination) are established through the Artist Terms of Service, which
constitute the Artist's documented instruction; they are not concluded as separate
per-Artist agreements.

Where Inklee and an Artist jointly determine the purposes and means of processing the
same Client data, they act as joint controllers within the meaning of Article 26 GDPR.
The essence of that arrangement is disclosed to Clients in the Platform privacy notice.

Stripe is an independent controller in respect of the Connected Account's identity
verification and KYC/AML data, which it processes and retains under its own legal
obligations, and is a processor in respect of transaction data processed on Inklee's
instruction, in each case as set out in the Stripe Data Processing Agreement.

## 2. Lawful bases

Artist account data is processed on the basis of contractual necessity (Article
6(1)(b)). Platform security, audit, and abuse-prevention data is processed on the basis
of Inklee's legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)). Retained financial and accounting
records are processed on the basis of compliance with a legal obligation (Article
6(1)(c)), namely Estonian accounting and tax law. Client reference images processed on
the Artist's behalf rely on the Artist's lawful basis; where any such image reveals
data within a special category, the condition in Article 9(2)(a) (explicit consent),
captured at the point of upload, applies.

## 3. Right to erasure and outstanding financial obligations

On a valid deletion request, whether made in-app or otherwise, Inklee erases the
Artist's personal data and the Client personal data that Inklee controls, save for data
that Inklee is required or permitted to retain under Article 17(3) GDPR.

Erasure of the Artist's profile and personal data is not conditioned on the prior
resolution of outstanding financial obligations. Where a Deposit remains unresolved at
the time of a deletion request, Inklee erases or pseudonymises the Artist's identifying
data and retains only the minimal financial record necessary for compliance with a
legal obligation (Article 17(3)(b)) and for the establishment, exercise, or defence of
legal claims (Article 17(3)(e)), while preserving the Client's route to a refund until
the financial matter is resolved. A general suspension of erasure pending financial
resolution is not applied.

Eligibility for deletion is determined by the state of any unresolved Deposit and not
solely by the balance held in the Connected Account. Where a Deposit has been taken for
a service not yet delivered, the deletion routine treats the associated booking as
unresolved regardless of the current Stripe balance, and preserves the Client's claim
and refund record accordingly.

The in-app deletion is both initiated and completed within the application, satisfying
Apple App Store Guideline 5.1.1(v); any financial resolution is handled without an
indefinite hard block on completion.

## 4. Financial and tax-record retention

Because the Artist is merchant of record for each Deposit, the Deposit principal is the
Artist's revenue and not Inklee's. Inklee's accounting source documents relate to its
own Platform Fee revenue, and Inklee retains the records necessary to substantiate that
revenue.

The retained financial record comprises: the Platform Fee amount; the Deposit amount as
the basis on which the fee was calculated; the currency; the Stripe payment-intent or
checkout-session identifier; the transaction status; the relevant timestamps; and the
internal booking or order identifier. It excludes Client email, Client handle or name,
free-text booking answers, and Client notes. Goods line-item detail is not retained;
where tax itemisation requires it, line items are retained as product descriptor and
price only, without Client personal data.

The retention period is seven years from the end of the financial year in which the
transaction was recorded, in accordance with § 12 of the Estonian Accounting Act. The
scheduled purge of retained records is keyed to the end of the relevant financial year.

## 5. Status of retained data

The retained financial record is pseudonymised data, not anonymised data: the Stripe
identifiers and internal identifiers permit re-identification by reference to Stripe
records or to the deletion record described in section 8. It therefore remains personal
data within the scope of the GDPR and is processed under Article 6(1)(c). It is
described and handled as pseudonymised data in all systems and documentation.

## 6. Client-uploaded reference images

Client-uploaded reference images are processed by Inklee as processor on the Artist's
behalf. They are stored with restricted access and encryption at rest, and are retained
no longer than necessary; an existing rule deletes such images thirty days after a
booking is rejected or cancelled.

These images are not biometric data processed for the purpose of uniquely identifying a
natural person, and Article 9 is not engaged on that basis. Where an image reveals data
concerning health or another special category, the Article 9(2)(a) explicit-consent
condition obtained at upload applies, and the image is handled subject to
special-category safeguards.

A data protection impact assessment under Article 35 GDPR is completed before release.
Its scope covers the reference images, the financial-record retention, the
erasure-handling logic in section 3, and the international transfer in section 7.

On deletion of an Artist's account, the Client reference images are deleted in
accordance with Article 28(3)(g). Where a Client has an unresolved transaction, the
Client's claim and refund route is preserved before deletion.

## 7. Connected Account, KYC data, and international transfer

The Connected Account's KYC personal data (legal name, date of birth, address, bank
account or IBAN, and uploaded identity documents) is held by Stripe as independent
controller under its anti-money-laundering and know-your-customer obligations. Inklee is
not required to force deletion or disconnection of the Connected Account at the time of
account deletion; Inklee retains only the Connected Account identifier, as pseudonymous
data, for the period in section 4.

The processing of personal data by Stripe in the United States is governed by the Stripe
Data Processing Agreement, which incorporates the Standard Contractual Clauses and
Stripe's certification under the EU–US Data Privacy Framework. This transfer mechanism
is recorded in Inklee's Article 30 records and disclosed in the Platform privacy notice.

Deletion of the Connected Account is scheduled at the end of the retention period in
section 4 and requires a zero balance.

## 8. Pseudonymous deletion and audit records

Inklee retains a pseudonymous deletion record (a random identifier, with no name, email,
or other personal data) and a moderation log, for the purposes of platform security and
abuse prevention, on the basis of Article 6(1)(f). These records are retained for
twenty-four months, consistent with the audit-log retention disclosed in the Platform
privacy notice, except where a record is linked to a retained financial record, in which
case it follows the period in section 4. Indefinite retention is not applied.

## 9. Confirmation of intent

Account deletion is confirmed by the user typing "DELETE" on a dedicated screen within
the authenticated session, and by re-authentication (re-entry of credentials or
re-completion of Sign in with Apple) immediately before the irreversible operation is
executed. This evidences informed intent and guards against unauthorised deletion.

## 10. Privacy-notice disclosures

The Platform privacy notice is updated, before release, to reflect the behaviour set out
above. The following disclosures are made, replacing the existing "plus 30 days"
formulation:

> **Deletion of your account.** When you delete your account, we erase your profile and
> the personal data we hold about you and about the clients whose details you entered,
> except where we are required or permitted by law to retain certain records. We retain a
> pseudonymised record of fee and transaction data (amounts, currency, payment references,
> and dates, without client names or contact details) for seven years from the end of the
> relevant financial year, to meet our accounting and tax obligations under Estonian law.
> Where a client has paid a deposit that has not been resolved, we keep the limited records
> needed to allow that deposit to be refunded and to protect the parties' legal claims.

> **Payments and identity data.** Payments and identity verification are handled by Stripe.
> Stripe holds the identity and KYC information of payment accounts as an independent
> controller and retains it under its own anti-money-laundering obligations, including after
> your account with us is deleted. Stripe may process this data outside the European Economic
> Area; such transfers are protected by the Standard Contractual Clauses and Stripe's EU–US
> Data Privacy Framework certification.

> **Security records.** We keep a pseudonymous record that an account was deleted, and
> related security logs, for up to twenty-four months to prevent abuse and to demonstrate
> that deletion took place.

## 11. Implementation requirements

| Ref | Requirement | Setting |
|---|---|---|
| 3 | Erasure not blocked on financial resolution | Replace "block-everything" with: erase/pseudonymise Artist PII, retain minimal financial + claim record, preserve refund route |
| 3 | Deletion eligibility | Determined by unresolved-Deposit state, not Stripe balance alone; undelivered-service Deposit = unresolved even at zero balance |
| 4 | Retained field set | Allowlist: fee amount, deposit amount, currency, Stripe payment-intent/checkout-session ID, status, timestamps, internal booking/order UUID. Strip all Client PII and free text |
| 4 | Retention period | 7 years from end of financial year; scheduled purge keyed to financial-year-end |
| 5 | Data labelling | Label retained set as "pseudonymised" throughout code and schema; treat as in-scope personal data |
| 6 | Reference images | Retain existing 30-day post-cancellation deletion; encryption at rest + restricted access; explicit-consent gate at upload where special-category |
| 6 | DPIA | Completed and signed off before release; gates the launch |
| 7 | Connected Account | Retain account pointer only; schedule account deletion at window-end (zero balance required); do not force-disconnect at deletion time |
| 7 | Transfer record | Record Stripe DPA + SCCs/DPF in Article 30 register; disclose in privacy notice |
| 8 | Audit/tombstone | Time-box to 24 months (or section-4 period where linked to a financial record); remove any indefinite retention |
| 9 | Intent capture | Type-to-confirm "DELETE" + re-authentication before execution |
| 10 | Privacy notice | Publish section-10 clauses; remove "plus 30 days" wording before release |
